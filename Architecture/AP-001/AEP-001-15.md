# 1. Purpose

This document establishes the initial Architecture Decision Register for 86-vibe.

Architecture Decision Records (ADRs) preserve the rationale behind significant architectural decisions and provide traceability throughout the evolution of the platform.

This document records the initial approved architectural decisions arising from Architecture Package AP-001.

Detailed ADR documents SHALL be maintained separately within the Architecture Decision Record repository.

---

# 2. ADR Objectives

The Architecture Decision Record process SHALL:

- preserve architectural knowledge
- document significant decisions
- support future maintainability
- improve engineering transparency
- reduce repeated architectural discussions
- provide historical traceability

ADRs are intended to explain *why* a decision was made rather than simply *what* was implemented.

---

# 3. ADR Principles

The following principles apply to all ADRs.

## ADRP-001 Permanence

Approved ADRs SHALL remain part of the permanent architectural history.

---

## ADRP-002 Immutability

Approved ADRs SHALL NOT be modified retrospectively.

Where a decision changes, a new ADR SHALL supersede the previous decision.

---

## ADRP-003 Traceability

Every significant architectural change SHOULD reference one or more ADRs.

---

## ADRP-004 Simplicity

Each ADR SHOULD describe a single architectural decision.

---

## ADRP-005 Accessibility

ADRs SHALL remain accessible to all contributors.

---

# 4. ADR Lifecycle

Every Architecture Decision Record SHALL follow the lifecycle below.

```text
Proposal
    │
    ▼
Architecture Review
    │
    ▼
Approval
    │
    ▼
Implementation
    │
    ▼
Historical Record
```

Once approved, an ADR becomes part of the permanent architectural history.

Subsequent decisions SHALL reference earlier ADRs where appropriate.

# 6. Future ADR Management

Future ADRs SHALL be created when changes affect:

- platform architecture
- repository strategy
- governance
- technology strategy
- AI architecture
- integration architecture
- engineering standards
- security architecture

Minor implementation decisions SHOULD NOT require ADRs unless they introduce architectural significance.

Every new ADR SHALL:

- have a unique identifier
- describe the decision
- record the rationale
- identify consequences
- identify superseded ADRs where applicable

---

# 7. Summary

The Architecture Decision Register establishes the permanent architectural memory of 86-vibe.

By recording significant architectural decisions and preserving their rationale, the platform supports long-term maintainability, transparency and governance.

Future architectural evolution SHALL build upon this decision history rather than replacing it.

Every significant architectural change SHALL be documented through the ADR process defined within this document.
