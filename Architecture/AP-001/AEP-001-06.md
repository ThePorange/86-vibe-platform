# 1. Purpose

This document defines the roles responsible for delivering, governing and operating projects created using 86-vibe.

Roles represent responsibilities rather than individuals or software components.

A role MAY be fulfilled by:

- a human
- an AI agent
- an external system
- a combination of the above

The implementation of a role MAY evolve over time without changing its responsibilities.

---

# 2. Role Model

86-vibe separates responsibilities into distinct roles to improve clarity, accountability and scalability.

Each role SHALL have:

- clearly defined responsibilities
- defined authority
- expected deliverables
- measurable outcomes

No role SHALL approve its own work where an independent review is required.

# 3. Governance Responsibilities

## Product Owner

The Product Owner is accountable for the success of the product.

Responsibilities include:

- defining product vision
- prioritising work
- approving architecture
- approving releases
- accepting completed stories
- managing the roadmap
- balancing scope, cost and value

Authority:

Final product decisions.

---

## Chief Systems Architect

The Chief Systems Architect is responsible for the overall architecture of the platform.

Responsibilities include:

- architecture design
- technical governance
- architecture reviews
- approving ADRs
- defining engineering standards
- ensuring architectural consistency

Authority:

Architecture approval.

---

# 10. Summary

The role model defined in this document establishes clear accountability for every major engineering activity within 86-vibe.

By separating responsibilities into stable roles, the platform remains independent of specific tools, AI providers and implementation technologies.

Future AI agents SHALL implement these roles rather than redefine them.

This approach enables the platform to evolve while preserving governance, accountability and engineering consistency.
