# 1. Purpose

This document defines the public implementation contract for the Health Monitoring Service.

The Health Monitoring Service is the authoritative platform component responsible for providing a unified view of the operational health, readiness, and liveness of the 86-vibe platform and its managed services.

Its purpose is to provide deterministic health assessment through a single public interface while remaining independent of business logic and individual service implementations.

This document extends the approved architecture.

It SHALL NOT modify the architectural behaviour defined by:

- AEP-002-17 Service Lifecycle & Dependency Management Specification
- AEP-002-16 Platform Bootstrap Sequence Specification
- AEP-002-06 Logging & Diagnostics Specification
- AEP-002-18 Platform Reference Implementation Specification

---

# 2. Scope

This specification defines:

- health monitoring responsibilities
- health probe registration
- readiness evaluation
- liveness evaluation
- health aggregation
- dependency contracts
- lifecycle behaviour
- extension requirements
- testing requirements

Implementation details are intentionally excluded.

---

# 3. Service Overview

The Health Monitoring Service provides centralized operational health monitoring.

Its responsibilities include:

- collecting service health
- aggregating platform health
- exposing readiness status
- exposing liveness status
- publishing health summaries
- supporting operational diagnostics

The Health Monitoring Service SHALL be the authoritative source of runtime health information.

# 5. Responsibilities

The Health Monitoring Service SHALL:

- register health providers
- evaluate health checks
- aggregate service health
- expose platform readiness
- expose platform liveness
- report degraded services
- provide deterministic health results

The Health Monitoring Service SHALL NOT:

- initialize platform services
- restart services
- perform business logic
- execute validation
- modify service state

---

# 6. Public Interface

The service SHALL expose operations equivalent to the following logical interface.

```text
initialize()

register(provider)

unregister(provider)

check()

readiness()

liveness()

status()

health()

shutdown()
```

Equivalent language-specific implementations are permitted provided externally observable behaviour remains identical.

---

# 7. Lifecycle Contract

Initialization SHALL occur after:

- Bootstrap Service
- Service Lifecycle Manager

Initialization sequence:

1. Construct service
2. Register mandatory platform probes
3. Initialize health aggregation
4. Publish monitoring availability
5. Accept provider registrations

Shutdown SHALL release registered probes and internal resources.

---

# 8. Dependency Contract

The Health Monitoring Service SHALL depend upon:

- Configuration Service
- Logging Service
- Service Lifecycle Manager
- Service Registry

The Health Monitoring Service SHALL NOT depend upon:

- Repository Service
- Prompt Management Service
- AI Provider Service
- MCP Client Service
- Validation Service

Health providers SHALL communicate only through the published interface.

---

# 9. Health Provider Contract

Each health provider SHALL expose logical operations equivalent to:

```text
name()

check()
```

A health provider SHALL return a standardized health result.

Providers SHALL NOT modify platform state while executing health checks.

---

# 10. Health Result Contract

Each health result SHALL include logical information equivalent to:

- service identifier
- health status
- timestamp
- summary message
- optional diagnostic details
- execution duration

Health results SHALL be immutable.

---

# 11. Platform Health Contract

The platform SHALL expose one overall health status.

Overall platform health SHALL be derived from registered mandatory services.

The platform SHALL report one of the following states.

| Status | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Healthy | All mandatory services operational |
| Degraded | One or more non-critical services degraded |
| Unhealthy | One or more mandatory services unavailable |
| Unknown | Health assessment incomplete |

Status definitions SHALL remain stable.

---

# 12. Readiness Contract

Readiness SHALL indicate whether the platform is capable of accepting work.

The platform SHALL be considered ready only when:

- bootstrap completed successfully
- mandatory services are healthy
- dependency checks have passed

---

# 13. Liveness Contract

Liveness SHALL indicate whether the platform process remains operational.

Liveness SHALL NOT require every service to be healthy.

Temporary service degradation SHALL NOT automatically indicate liveness failure.

---

# 14. Aggregation Contract

The Health Monitoring Service SHALL aggregate health deterministically.

Aggregation SHALL:

- include mandatory services
- include optional services when available
- preserve individual health results
- produce a single platform summary

---

# 15. Error Contract

Errors SHALL be classified consistently.

| Error Type | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Provider Failure | Health provider execution failed |
| Unknown Provider | Provider not registered |
| Timeout | Health check exceeded timeout |
| Aggregation Failure | Platform health could not be calculated |
| Internal Error | Unexpected monitoring failure |

Implementation-specific exceptions SHALL remain encapsulated.

---

# 16. Logging Contract

The Health Monitoring Service SHALL:

- log initialization
- log provider registration
- log degraded services
- log unhealthy platform states
- log monitoring failures

Routine successful health checks SHOULD generate minimal logging.

---

# 17. Security Requirements

The Health Monitoring Service SHALL:

- avoid exposing sensitive configuration
- sanitize diagnostic information
- validate provider registrations
- fail securely

Operational diagnostics SHALL exclude secrets.

---

# 18. Performance Expectations

The Health Monitoring Service SHALL:

- minimize monitoring overhead
- support concurrent health checks where appropriate
- avoid unnecessary polling
- complete routine health evaluation efficiently

Performance optimizations SHALL preserve deterministic results.

---

# 19. Thread Safety

The Health Monitoring Service SHALL support concurrent:

- provider registrations
- health checks
- readiness requests
- liveness requests

Concurrent operations SHALL preserve result integrity.

---

# 20. Extension Contract

Future health providers SHALL:

- implement the public health provider interface
- return standardized health results
- support deterministic execution
- remain independent of consumers

Existing consumers SHALL remain unaffected by additional providers.

---

# 21. Consumer Contract

Consumers SHALL:

- obtain platform health exclusively through the Health Monitoring Service
- avoid querying service internals directly
- treat reported health as authoritative

Consumers SHALL NOT aggregate health independently.

---

# 22. Testing Requirements

Testing SHALL verify:

- initialization
- provider registration
- health aggregation
- readiness reporting
- liveness reporting
- degraded service reporting
- concurrent execution
- shutdown
- error handling

Mock health providers SHOULD be used for deterministic testing.

---

# 23. AI Implementation Guidance

AI implementation agents SHALL:

- preserve the public monitoring interface
- implement health providers independently
- avoid embedding monitoring logic within unrelated services
- preserve deterministic health aggregation
- maintain standardized health result structures

---

# 24. Backward Compatibility

Future releases SHALL preserve:

- public service interface
- health status definitions
- readiness semantics
- liveness semantics
- aggregation behaviour

Breaking changes SHALL require:

- architecture review
- semantic version increment
- approved ADR where applicable

---

# 25. Conformance Criteria

This service contract is satisfied when:

- platform health is available exclusively through the Health Monitoring Service
- readiness and liveness conform to this specification
- health aggregation is deterministic
- provider implementations remain encapsulated
- concurrency requirements are satisfied

This document is complete when:

- the Health Monitoring Service public interface is fully defined
- lifecycle behaviour is specified
- dependency contracts are established
- readiness and liveness contracts are documented
- extension requirements are defined
- implementation teams can implement the Health Monitoring Service without further architectural clarification
