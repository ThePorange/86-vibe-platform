# 1. Purpose

This document defines the architectural principles that govern the design and evolution of 86-vibe.

These principles are intended to remain stable over the lifetime of the platform and SHALL be used when evaluating architectural decisions, feature requests, implementation approaches and technology choices.

Where conflicts arise, these principles SHALL take precedence unless an approved Architecture Decision Record (ADR) explicitly authorises an exception.

---

# 2. Design Philosophy

86-vibe is built upon a simple philosophy:

> **Software engineering is a discipline, not a coding activity.**

The platform exists to improve the engineering process rather than simply accelerate code generation.

Artificial Intelligence SHALL augment engineering practices, not replace them.

Every feature SHOULD strengthen the engineering process.

---

# 3. Foundational Principles

## DP-01 Human Ownership

Humans SHALL retain ownership of:

- Product vision
- Product roadmap
- Prioritisation
- Architecture approval
- Production releases

AI SHALL assist rather than replace accountable decision makers.

---

## DP-02 Architecture Before Implementation

No implementation SHALL begin before an approved architectural approach exists.

Implementation without architectural intent introduces unnecessary technical debt.

---

## DP-03 Documentation First

Documentation SHALL be considered a first-class engineering artefact.

Every significant engineering activity SHOULD update the relevant documentation.

Documentation SHALL evolve alongside the software.

---

## DP-04 Traceability

Every engineering artefact SHOULD be traceable.

The preferred traceability chain is:

Vision

↓

Requirement

↓

Architecture

↓

Epic

↓

Story

↓

Implementation

↓

Testing

↓

Release

---

## DP-05 Reuse Before Creation

Existing capabilities SHOULD be reused before creating new solutions.

Duplication SHOULD be avoided unless justified through an ADR.

---

# 4. Engineering Principles

## DP-06 Simplicity

The simplest solution that satisfies the requirements SHOULD be preferred.

Complexity SHALL require explicit justification.

---

## DP-07 Modularity

Capabilities SHOULD be modular and independently maintainable.

Strong coupling SHOULD be avoided.

---

## DP-08 Automation

Repetitive engineering activities SHOULD be automated wherever practical.

Automation SHALL increase consistency rather than reduce governance.

---

## DP-09 Quality

Quality SHALL take precedence over development speed.

No feature is complete until it satisfies its acceptance criteria.

---

## DP-10 Continuous Improvement

Every completed project SHOULD contribute improvements back into the platform.

Lessons learned SHALL become reusable engineering knowledge.

---

# 5. AI Principles

## DP-11 Specialised AI Agents

AI responsibilities SHALL be divided between specialised agents.

Each agent SHALL have:

- clearly defined responsibilities
- approved tools
- expected inputs
- expected outputs
- measurable outcomes

---

## DP-12 Vendor Neutrality

86-vibe SHALL avoid unnecessary dependence upon specific AI providers.

Model selection SHALL remain configurable.

---

## DP-13 Cost Awareness

AI usage SHOULD balance capability against operational cost.

Tasks SHOULD use the least capable model that satisfies quality requirements.

---

## DP-14 Explainability

AI-generated outputs SHOULD be understandable.

Important engineering decisions SHOULD include supporting rationale.

---

## DP-15 Human Review

Critical engineering decisions SHALL require human approval.

Automation SHALL never remove accountability.

---

# 6. Product Principles

## DP-16 Platform Over Product

86-vibe SHALL remain an engineering platform.

Application-specific functionality SHALL belong within application repositories.

---

## DP-17 Integration Over Replacement

Where mature tools already exist, integration SHOULD be preferred over replacement.

86-vibe coordinates tools rather than competing with them.

---

## DP-18 Extensibility

The platform SHOULD support future expansion without major redesign.

Extension points SHOULD be preferred over hard-coded behaviours.

---

## DP-19 Knowledge Preservation

Engineering knowledge SHALL outlive:

- AI models
- IDEs
- contributors
- technology changes

Knowledge SHALL remain accessible through documentation.

---

## DP-20 Self-Application

86-vibe SHALL be developed using its own engineering methodology.

The platform SHALL demonstrate the practices it recommends.

---

# 7. Governance Principles

## DP-21 Version Control

Every engineering artefact SHALL be version controlled.

---

## DP-22 Architecture Decisions

Significant architectural changes SHALL be documented using ADRs.

---

## DP-23 Approval Gates

Major lifecycle stages SHALL include explicit approval gates.

---

## DP-24 Standards

Shared engineering standards SHALL apply across all projects created using 86-vibe.

---

## DP-25 Measurable Outcomes

Engineering improvements SHOULD be measurable using defined metrics.

---

# 8. Decision Framework

When evaluating a proposal, reviewers SHOULD consider the following questions:

1. Does this support the product vision?
2. Does this improve software engineering?
3. Does this preserve simplicity?
4. Does this improve maintainability?
5. Can this be reused across projects?
6. Does it duplicate an existing capability?
7. Does it increase technical debt?
8. Does it preserve vendor neutrality?
9. Is it measurable?
10. Is an ADR required?

If multiple principles conflict, the Product Owner and Chief Systems Architect SHALL determine the appropriate balance and record the rationale in an ADR.

---

# 9. Summary

These design principles establish the constitutional framework for 86-vibe.

They provide a consistent basis for evaluating architecture, implementation and governance decisions throughout the lifetime of the platform.

All future architecture packages, engineering standards and implementation work SHOULD align with these principles unless an approved ADR explicitly authorises an alternative approach.
